Heatwave Safety Tips

What Is a Heatwave?

A heatwave is a prolonged period of excessively hot weather, often combined with high humidity, that significantly exceeds the normal temperatures for a region. Heatwaves are among the deadliest natural hazards — they cause more deaths globally than floods, hurricanes, and tornadoes combined. Rising global temperatures driven by climate change are making heatwaves more frequent, longer-lasting, and more intense, affecting millions of people each year.

Warning Signs of Heat-Related Illness

  • Heat Cramps: Muscle pain or spasms in the abdomen, arms, or legs — usually the first sign of heat illness.
  • Heat Exhaustion: Heavy sweating, weakness, cold/pale/clammy skin, weak pulse, nausea, fainting.
  • Heat Stroke (Emergency 🚨): Body temperature above 39.4°C (103°F), hot/red/dry skin, rapid pulse, possible unconsciousness. Call emergency services immediately.
  • Dehydration: Dark yellow urine, dry mouth, dizziness, and reduced urination are early warning signs.

Before a Heatwave (Prepare)

  • Know the location of your nearest cooling center (library, mall, community hall).
  • Prepare an emergency kit with bottled water, electrolyte drinks, a battery fan, sunscreen, and a first aid manual.
  • Install reflective window coverings or blackout curtains to keep your home cooler.
  • Sign up for local weather alerts and heatwave warnings from your national weather service.
  • Check on elderly neighbours, young children, and people with chronic illness — they are most at risk.

During a Heatwave (Stay Safe)

  • Stay indoors during peak heat (11 AM – 4 PM) and use AC or fans.
  • Drink water regularly — don't wait until you feel thirsty.
  • Wear loose, lightweight, light-coloured clothing.
  • Take cool (not cold) showers to lower your body temperature.
  • Never leave children, elderly, or pets in parked vehicles.
  • Avoid alcohol and caffeine — they increase dehydration.
  • Don't do strenuous outdoor activity during peak heat hours.
  • Close curtains on sun-facing windows during the day.
