Hurricane & Typhoon Safety Tips

What Is a Hurricane / Typhoon?

Hurricanes and typhoons are the same type of storm — a powerful tropical cyclone with sustained winds exceeding 119 km/h (74 mph). They are called hurricanes in the Atlantic and eastern Pacific, and typhoons in the western Pacific. These storms form over warm ocean waters and can cause catastrophic damage through high winds, storm surges, and flooding.

Early Warning Signs

  • Rapidly dropping barometric pressure
  • Increasing wind speeds and direction shifts
  • Heavy rainfall and darkening skies
  • Unusually large ocean swells or storm surge warnings
  • Official hurricane/typhoon watch or warning issued

Before a Hurricane (Prepare)

  • Board up windows and reinforce doors
  • Prepare a 72-hour emergency kit (water, food, medicines, documents)
  • Know your evacuation route and local shelter locations
  • Fill up fuel tanks and charge all devices
  • Trim trees and secure loose outdoor objects
  • Review first aid procedures

During a Hurricane (Stay Safe)

  • Stay indoors away from windows
  • Go to an interior room on the lowest floor
  • Listen to emergency broadcasts
  • If evacuating, leave early and follow official routes
  • Don't go outside during the eye — the storm returns
  • Don't drive through flooded roads
  • Don't use candles near gas lines
  • Don't use generators indoors
