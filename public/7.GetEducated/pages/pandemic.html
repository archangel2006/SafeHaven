Pandemic & Disease Outbreak Safety

What Is a Pandemic?

A pandemic is the worldwide spread of an infectious disease that affects large numbers of people across multiple countries or continents. It can spread rapidly through close contact, contaminated surfaces, or airborne transmission depending on the disease. Public health measures help reduce transmission and protect communities.

Early Warning Signs

  • Reports of increasing local infection cases
  • Government or health department advisories
  • Unusual clusters of fever, cough, or respiratory illness

Before an Outbreak (Prepare)

  • Keep soap, sanitizer, and disinfectants available
  • Maintain a first aid and medicine supply
  • Store quality face masks if recommended
  • Stay up to date with recommended vaccinations
  • Create a family emergency communication plan

During a Pandemic (Stay Safe)

  • Wash hands frequently with soap
  • Wear a mask when recommended
  • Maintain physical distancing in crowded places
  • Stay home if you feel unwell
  • Avoid crowded gatherings during outbreaks
  • Don't spread unverified health information
  • Avoid close contact with sick individuals
  • Seek medical care if symptoms become severe
