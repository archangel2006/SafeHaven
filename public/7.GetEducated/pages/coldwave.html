Cold Wave Safety Tips

What Is a Cold Wave?

A cold wave is a prolonged period of extremely low temperatures, often accompanied by strong winds, frost, snowfall, and icy conditions. Cold waves can cause hypothermia, frostbite, health complications, transportation disruptions, and damage to crops and livestock.

Early Warning Signs

  • Rapid drop in temperature
  • Weather department cold wave warnings
  • Frost formation on surfaces
  • Strong cold winds and wind chill effects
  • Snowfall or icy road conditions

Before a Cold Wave (Prepare)

  • Insulate your home and seal doors and windows
  • Keep warm clothing, blankets, and winter gear ready
  • Prepare an emergency kit with food, water, batteries, and medicines
  • Charge devices and prepare for possible power outages
  • Arrange shelter for pets and livestock
  • Monitor weather forecasts and cold wave alerts

During a Cold Wave (Stay Safe)

  • Dress in multiple warm layers
  • Stay indoors whenever possible
  • Drink warm fluids and stay hydrated
  • Check on elderly family members and neighbors
  • Do not stay outside for long periods
  • Avoid alcohol when exposed to cold weather
  • Avoid unnecessary travel during icy conditions
  • Avoid direct exposure to freezing winds

After a Cold Wave (Recovery)

  • Inspect your home for frozen pipes and structural damage
  • Check water supply systems for leaks
  • Be cautious of lingering ice on roads and walkways
  • Seek medical attention for frostbite or hypothermia symptoms
  • Assist vulnerable community members during recovery
