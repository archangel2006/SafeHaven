Building Collapse Safety Tips

What Is an Earthquake?

A building collapse occurs when a structure partially or completely fails due to poor construction, aging materials, natural disasters, explosions, fires, or structural damage. Building collapses can happen suddenly and may cause serious injuries or loss of life.

Early Warning Signs

  • Large cracks in walls or ceilings
  • Leaning walls or pillars
  • Sagging floors or roofs
  • Unusual creaking sounds

Before an Earthquake (Prepare)

  • Inspect buildings regularly
  • Report structural damage immediately
  • Keep an emergency kit ready
  • Learn evacuation routes

During an Earthquake (Stay Safe)

  • Protect your head and neck
  • Take cover under sturdy furniture
  • Stay calm and avoid panic
  • Do not use elevators
  • Do not run toward exits during collapse
  • Do not re-enter damaged structures

After a Building Collapse

  • Check yourself and others for injuries.
  • Call emergency services immediately.
  • Avoid damaged buildings.
  • Watch for gas leaks and electrical hazards.
  • Follow instructions from authorities.
