Building collapse Safety Tips

What Is a Building Collapse?

A building collapse is the sudden, partial, or total structural failure of a structure, rendering it unable to support its own weight or external loads

Early Warning Signs

  • New or widening cracks in walls, ceilings, or columns.
  • Floors that sag, slope, or feel uneven.
  • Doors and windows that suddenly become difficult to open or close.

Before a Building Collapse (Preparation)

  • Learn the building's emergency exits and evacuation routes
  • Report cracks, sagging floors, or other structural damage immediately
  • Prepare an emergency kit with water, a flashlight, a first-aid kit, and essential supplies
  • Participate in emergency drills and follow building safety guidelines

During a Building Collapse (Stay Safe)

  • Take cover under a sturdy table or desk if possible.
  • Protect your head and neck with your arms.
  • Cover your nose and mouth to reduce dust inhalation.
  • Don't use elevators.
  • Don't run toward exits if debris is falling.
  • Stay calm and wait for the collapse to stop before evacuating.
