Landslide Safety

What is a Landslide?

A landslide is the movement of rock, soil, debris, or earth down a slope. Landslides are commonly triggered by heavy rainfall, earthquakes, volcanic activity, deforestation, or human construction activities.

Safety Measures During a Landslide

  • Stay alert during heavy rainfall in hilly regions.
  • Move immediately to higher and safer ground if a landslide warning is issued.
  • Avoid river valleys and steep slopes.
  • Never cross an active landslide area.
  • Keep an emergency kit ready with water, food, medicines and important documents.

Real-Life Landslide Case

The 2014 Oso Landslide in Washington, USA, caused significant destruction and loss of life, highlighting the importance of early warning systems and disaster preparedness.
